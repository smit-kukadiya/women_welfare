WHY STARTED A MAHILA HELPLINE YOJANA

             Scheme of Universalization of Women Helpline aims to provide 24 hours immediate and emergency response to women affected by violence through linking with appropriate authority such as police, One Stop Centre, hospital related to women across the country To provide information about government schemes.
             The Women Helpline will provide 24-hour emergency response to all women affected by violence, both in the public and private sectors. All existing emergency services such as Police (100), Fire (101), Women Helpline (1091), Hospital / Ambulance (102), Emergency Response Services (108), NALSA Helpline (15100) and Child Helpline (1098) for free legal service will be integrated with this Woman Helpline. The proposed women helpline will utilize the infrastructure of existing Chief Minister helpline in some states through 181 as well as 108 services.

REQUIRED NUMBERS FOR MAHILA HELPLINE YOJANA

  • Women's Helpline - 1091
  • Women's Helpline for Domestic Violence - 181
  • Police Helpline 100 National Commission For Women Helpline - 011-26942369,26944809
  • Student / Child Helpline Number - 1098
  • National Human Rights Commission(MADAD) - 91-11-24651330,14433

OBJECTIVES OF MAHILA HELPLINE YOJANA

  1. To provide toll free 24 hours telecommunication service to women affected by violence seeking support and information.
  2. To facilitate crisis and non-crisis interventions through referral to appropriate agencies like Police / Hospitals / Ambulance Services / District Legal Services Authority (DLSA) / Protection Officer (PO) / OSC.
  3. To provide information about appropriate support services, government schemes and programs available to a woman affected by violence, in her particular situation within the local area in which she lives or is employed.
  4. Creation and maintenance of a comprehensive referral database by helplines within your local area.
